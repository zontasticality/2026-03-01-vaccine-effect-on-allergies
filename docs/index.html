Vaccine Effect on Allergies

Prevalence and characteristics of peanut allergy in US adults
Christopher Warren, Dawn Lei, Scott Sicherer, Robert Schleimer, Ruchi Gupta · Journal of Allergy and Clinical Immunology, 147(6):2263-2270, 2021 · DOI: 10.1016/j.jaci.2020.11.046
scouted-only

Establishes the magnitude of the peanut allergy burden — ~1.8% convincing prevalence in US adults, ~800,000 with adult-onset. Provides context for the "how big is this problem" question but does not directly address vaccine causation.

The Allergy Epidemics: 1870-2010
Thomas A.E. Platts-Mills · Journal of Allergy and Clinical Immunology, 136(1):3-13, 2015 · DOI: 10.1016/j.jaci.2015.03.048
read-partialHIGH

Authoritative review tracing three sequential allergy epidemics. Attributes them primarily to lifestyle changes (hygiene, indoor living, skin exposure). Notes vaccine formulation changes (whole-cell → acellular pertussis in 1992) as potentially relevant to food allergy timing but emphasizes no proper controlled trial exists. Does NOT endorse vaccines as a primary cause.

Aluminum Adjuvant-Containing Vaccines in the Context of the Hygiene Hypothesis: A Risk Factor for Eosinophilia and Allergy in a Genetically Susceptible Subpopulation?
Terhune, Deth · International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health, 2018 · DOI: 10.3390/ijerph15050901
read-partialMODERATE-HIGH

Proposes that aluminum adjuvants trigger Th2-skewed immune responses without regulatory T cell activation, potentially increasing allergy risk in genetically susceptible children. Relies primarily on mouse models. Explicitly acknowledges this is a hypothesis requiring validation, not proven causation. 119 references.

Anaphylaxis to additives in vaccines
Vera Mahler, Ann-Christine Junker · Allergo Journal International, 2022 · DOI: 10.1007/s40629-022-00215-8
read-partialHIGH

Comprehensive review of allergenic vaccine excipients (gelatin, ovalbumin, casein, PEG, polysorbate 80). Documents anaphylaxis rates of 1-10 per million vaccinations. Key data: ovalbumin limit ≤1 µg/dose, bovine casein detected in nanogram amounts in DTaP. Distinguishes between rare anaphylactic reactions (well-documented) and the broader question of whether vaccines drive population-level allergy sensitization (not addressed).

Timing of routine infant vaccinations and risk of food allergy and eczema at one year of age
Venter et al. · Allergy, 71(4):541-549, 2016 · DOI: 10.1111/all.12830
scouted-onlyHIGH

Population-based cohort of 4,433 children. Delayed DTaP was NOT associated with food allergy (p=0.49) but WAS associated with reduced eczema (p=0.04) and eczema medication use (p=0.01).

Timeliness of DTaP-IPV-Hib Vaccination and Development of Atopic Dermatitis Between 4 Months and 1 Year of Age—Register-Based Cohort Study
Lise Gehrt et al. · JACI: In Practice, 2021 · DOI: 10.1016/j.jaip.2020.09.024
scouted-onlyHIGH

Massive cohort: 883,160 Danish children (1997-2012). Delayed DTaP associated with reduced atopic dermatitis: aRR 0.94 (0.91-0.97) for delayed first dose; aRR 0.88 (0.82-0.93) for delayed first and second doses. Dose-response relationship strengthens causal inference.

Whole-Cell Pertussis Vaccination and Decreased Risk of IgE-Mediated Food Allergy: A Nested Case-Control Study
Estcourt et al. · JACI: In Practice, 8(6):2004-2014, 2020 · DOI: 10.1016/j.jaip.2019.12.020
scouted-onlyVERY HIGH

Australian children born 1997-1999 during wP→aP transition. Infants receiving whole-cell pertussis (wP) had OR 0.77 (95% CI 0.62-0.95) for IgE-mediated food allergy vs. acellular pertussis (aP). Suggests vaccine *formulation* matters more than vaccination itself.

Vaccination and Allergic Disease: A Birth Cohort Study
Grüber et al. · American Journal of Public Health, 2003 · DOI: 10.1093/aje/kwh035
read-partialHIGH

29,238 children. Initial vaccine-allergy association vanished after adjusting for GP visit frequency. 83% of unvaccinated children were in the lowest quartile of doctor visits. Concluded the association was ascertainment bias: unvaccinated children simply got diagnosed less.

Vaccination Status and Health in Children and Adolescents (KiGGS)
Schmitz et al. · Deutsches Ärzteblatt International, 2011 · DOI: 10.3238/arztebl.2011.0099
read-partialHIGH

13,453 German children (1-17 years). Found NO statistically significant differences in atopic disorder prevalence between vaccinated and unvaccinated. Some raw numbers showed higher rates in vaccinated children for certain age groups but not after adjustment.

Effect of endotoxin and alum adjuvant vaccine on peanut allergy
Hurley, Tadrous · 2018 · DOI: 10.1016/j.jaci.2017.09.016
read-partialMODERATE

Mouse model. Alum/CpG adjuvant combination reduced peanut-induced allergic responses compared to alum alone. Suggests adjuvant formulation modifications could reduce allergy risk. Authors note: "modification of childhood vaccine adjuvant formulations may provide a method to reduce the development/severity of allergic disease."

Historical Comparisons of Morbidity and Mortality for Vaccine-Preventable Diseases in the United States
Roush, Murphy · JAMA, 2007 · DOI: 10.1001/jama.298.18.2155
scouted-only

Essential context for the risk-benefit analysis. Documents >92% decline in cases and ≥99% decline in deaths for diphtheria, mumps, pertussis, tetanus after vaccination. ~103 million cases prevented since 1924.
