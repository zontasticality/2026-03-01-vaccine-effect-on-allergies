# Research Notes — Vaccine Effect on Allergies

## 1. The Scale of the Allergy Problem

### Peanut allergy prevalence (Warren et al. 2021)
- [MEASURED] ~1.8% of US adults meet "convincing" criteria for peanut allergy
- [MEASURED] >17% of cases were adult-onset (developed after age 18), representing ~800,000 Americans
- [MY SYNTHESIS] This paper establishes the *magnitude* of the problem. It does not address causation. The adult-onset finding is interesting because it suggests ongoing sensitization processes, not just early childhood events.

### The three allergy epidemics (Platts-Mills 2015)
- [CLAIMED] Three sequential epidemics: hay fever (~1870), pediatric asthma (~1960), food allergy (~1990)
- [INFERRED] "sequential changes in lifestyle have led to increases in different forms of allergic disease" (Platts-Mills, JACI 2015, p. 3)
- [CLAIMED] Hay fever: explained by hygiene + agricultural changes increasing pollen exposure
- [CLAIMED] Asthma: driven by indoor lifestyle (TV watching → reduced deep breathing → BHR; increased indoor allergen exposure)
- [CLAIMED] Food allergy: skin sensitization pathway (peanut exposure through skin in absence of early oral exposure)
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Platts-Mills' framework is important because it shows the allergy epidemics have multiple, sequential causes tied to lifestyle — NOT a single cause. Vaccines are mentioned only peripherally.

### Platts-Mills on vaccines specifically
- [CLAIMED] "a proper controlled trial comparing the effects of cellular versus acellular pertussis vaccine on allergic disease has not yet been conducted" (Platts-Mills 2015, via PMC)
- [CLAIMED] The switch from whole-cell to acellular pertussis (1992 in the US) occurred around the same time as the food allergy epidemic acceleration
- [CLAIMED] BCG vaccination: early claims of allergy protection were "not confirmed in subsequent studies"
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Platts-Mills treats the pertussis vaccine formulation change as a *possible contributing factor* worthy of investigation, but NOT as a primary cause. His framework puts vastly more weight on indoor lifestyle, skin barrier dysfunction, and early feeding practices.

## 2. The Arumugham Hypothesis: Food Proteins in Vaccines

### Core claim (Arumugham 2015)
- [CLAIMED] "injecting a protein into animals or humans causes immune system sensitization to that protein" — citing Richet (Nobel 1913) and Wells (1908)
- [CLAIMED] Wells (1908) showed 50 nanograms of ovalbumin injected into guinea pigs caused sensitization
- [CLAIMED] "there are no specifications limiting allergen content in vaccines approved for use in the United States"
- [CLAIMED] IOM 2011 report stated vaccine antigens like gelatin and ovalbumin "do occasionally induce IgE-mediated sensitization in some individuals"

### Specific proteins identified by Arumugham
- Chicken egg (ovalbumin) — in influenza, MMR, yellow fever vaccines
- Gelatin — in MMR, varicella, influenza vaccines
- Casein (milk protein) — in DTaP manufacturing
- Soy — in various
- Polysorbate 80 — manufactured from various oils

### Evaluation of Arumugham's claims
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **The basic immunology is correct**: injecting proteins with adjuvants CAN cause IgE sensitization. This is well-established since Richet's Nobel Prize work (1913).
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **The regulatory gap claim appears correct**: Mahler & Junker (2022) confirm that bovine casein is detected in "nanogram amounts" in DTaP vaccines, and ovalbumin limits are ≤1 µg/dose.
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **The leap from "can cause" to "does cause the epidemic" is NOT supported**: Arumugham provides no epidemiological evidence, no dose-response data in humans, and no comparison of allergy rates by vaccination status or by specific vaccine exposure.
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **Publication quality is very poor**: Longdom/OMICS group is a known predatory publisher. No peer review of substance is guaranteed. This does NOT make the claims false, but it means they have not been subjected to rigorous scrutiny.

## 3. Mechanistic Evidence: Adjuvants and IgE

### Aluminum adjuvants and Th2 skewing (Terhune & Deth 2018)
- [CLAIMED] Aluminum adjuvants "do not induce an environment conducive to the induction of regulatory mechanisms"
- [INFERRED] Unlike chronic parasitic infections (which also trigger Th2 but also activate Tregs), aluminum adjuvants trigger Th2 responses WITHOUT regulatory T cell activation
- [CLAIMED] This Th2-skewing without immune regulation could increase allergy risk in genetically susceptible (atopic) children
- **Limitations explicitly stated by authors**: primarily mouse models, no direct human epidemiological evidence, presents hypothesis not proof

### Actual documented allergenic reactions to vaccine excipients (Mahler & Junker 2022)
- [MEASURED] Anaphylaxis rates: 1-10 per million vaccinations (historical vaccines)
- [MEASURED] COVID-19 vaccine anaphylaxis: 0.8-3.5 per million doses depending on formulation
- [MEASURED] Gelatin in vaccines has caused anaphylaxis; "one third of patients exhibiting IgE antibodies to gelatin"
- [MEASURED] Ovalbumin limit: ≤1 µg per vaccine dose (European Pharmacopoeia)
- [MEASURED] Bovine casein: detected in "nanogram amounts" in DTaP
- [MEASURED] 8 children with severe milk allergies experienced anaphylaxis following DTaP boosters (case series)
- [MY SYNTHESIS] This confirms that vaccine excipients CAN trigger IgE-mediated reactions in already-sensitized individuals. The question of whether vaccines *create* the sensitization in the first place remains separate.

### Mouse model: adjuvant formulation matters (Hurley & Tadrous 2018)
- [MEASURED] Alum/CpG combination "induced vaccine-specific Th1-immunity and allergic responses statistically similar to naïve mice" — i.e., it prevented the allergy-promoting effect of alum alone
- [INFERRED] "modification of childhood vaccine adjuvant formulations may provide a method to reduce the development/severity of allergic disease"
- [MY SYNTHESIS] This is important: even within the framework that adjuvants affect allergy, the solution would be *better adjuvants*, not skipping vaccines.

## 4. Epidemiological Evidence

### The strongest evidence: whole-cell vs. acellular pertussis

#### Estcourt et al. 2020 (nested case-control, Australia)
- [MEASURED] Children receiving whole-cell pertussis (wP) as first dose: OR 0.77 (95% CI 0.62-0.95) for IgE-mediated food allergy vs. acellular pertussis (aP)
- [MY SYNTHESIS] This is a 23% reduction in food allergy odds with whole-cell vaccine. Methodologically strong: used the natural experiment of Australia's 1997-1999 transition period.

#### Estcourt et al. 2024 (cohort, NSW Australia, N=218,093)
- [MEASURED] Food anaphylaxis hospitalization rate: wP = 3.5 vs aP = 5.1 per 100,000 child-years
- [MEASURED] Adjusted hazard ratio: 0.47 (95% CI 0.26-0.83)
- [MEASURED] Venom anaphylaxis: no significant difference (HR 0.92) — suggesting the effect is specific to food allergy, not generalized immune dysregulation
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **This is the single strongest piece of evidence in this entire investigation.** The 53% reduction in food anaphylaxis hospitalization with whole-cell vaccine, with no effect on venom anaphylaxis, is specific and biologically plausible. The specificity to food (not venom) anaphylaxis argues against confounding.

### DTaP timing and atopic dermatitis

#### Gehrt et al. 2021 (Denmark, N=883,160)
- [MEASURED] Delayed first DTaP dose: aRR 0.94 (0.91-0.97) for atopic dermatitis
- [MEASURED] Delayed first AND second doses: aRR 0.88 (0.82-0.93)
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Dose-response relationship supports causality. But the effect is **small** (6-12% relative risk reduction). And this is atopic dermatitis, not food allergy.

#### Venter et al. 2016 (Melbourne HealthNuts, N=4,433)
- [MEASURED] Delayed DTaP: NOT associated with food allergy (p=0.49)
- [MEASURED] Delayed DTaP: associated with reduced eczema (p=0.04)
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Directly contradicts the hypothesis that DTaP timing drives food allergy. Supports a modest eczema effect.

### Vaccinated vs. unvaccinated comparisons

#### KiGGS (Germany, N=13,453, Schmitz et al. 2011)
- [MEASURED] No statistically significant differences in atopic disorder prevalence between vaccinated and unvaccinated after adjustment
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Largest well-conducted study of its kind. Strongly argues against a large vaccine-allergy effect.

#### Grüber et al. 2003 (West Midlands, N=29,238)
- [MEASURED] Initial association between vaccination and allergic disease
- [MEASURED] Association disappeared after adjusting for GP visit frequency
- [MEASURED] 83% of unvaccinated children were in the lowest quartile of doctor visits
- [INFERRED] "currently recommended routine vaccinations are not a risk factor for asthma or eczema" — concluded to be **ascertainment bias**
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **Critical methodological lesson**: unvaccinated children see doctors less, so they get diagnosed less. Any comparison must account for healthcare utilization.

#### Mawson et al. 2017 (homeschool, N=666)
- [CLAIMED] Higher allergy rates in vaccinated children
- [MY SYNTHESIS] **Methodologically worthless**: parental recall only, no medical records, self-selected sample, retracted from Frontiers, reviewed by a chiropractor, published in predatory journal. Cannot be given weight.

## 5. The Risk-Benefit Context

### Vaccine-preventable disease burden (Roush & Murphy 2007, JAMA)
- [MEASURED] Pre-vaccine era annual US cases: measles ~530,000 (reported; actual estimated 3-4 million), diphtheria ~21,053, pertussis ~200,752
- [MEASURED] Pre-vaccine era annual US deaths: measles ~440, diphtheria ~1,822, pertussis ~4,034
- [MEASURED] Post-vaccine: >92% decline in cases, ≥99% decline in deaths
- [MEASURED] ~103 million cases prevented since 1924

### Putting the food allergy risk in context
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Even taking the most generous interpretation of the wP vs aP data: the acellular pertussis vaccine is associated with food anaphylaxis at 5.1 per 100,000 child-years (vs 3.5 for whole-cell). The **absolute increase** attributable to the acellular formulation is ~1.6 per 100,000 child-years.
- [MY SYNTHESIS] Pre-vaccine pertussis killed ~4,034 Americans per year when the population was ~120 million, i.e., ~3.4 per 100,000.
- [MY SYNTHESIS] The pertussis mortality rate was approximately **twice** the attributable food anaphylaxis hospitalization rate from the acellular formulation. And pertussis deaths are deaths, while food anaphylaxis hospitalizations are mostly survivable.

## 6. Key Distinctions That Matter

### "Vaccines cause allergies" is not a single claim — it's at least four:

1. **Vaccine excipients can trigger anaphylaxis in already-sensitized individuals** — TRUE, well-documented, rare (1-10 per million)

2. **The acellular pertussis vaccine is associated with higher food allergy risk than the whole-cell formulation** — PROBABLY TRUE, supported by strong epidemiological evidence (Estcourt 2020, 2024)

3. **Aluminum adjuvants promote Th2 skewing that increases allergy risk in susceptible individuals** — PLAUSIBLE, supported by mouse models and mechanistic reasoning, but lacks human epidemiological confirmation

4. **Trace food proteins in vaccines are a primary driver of the food allergy epidemic** — UNSUBSTANTIATED, based on mechanistic speculation and published only in predatory journals, contradicted by large population studies showing no overall vaccine-allergy association
