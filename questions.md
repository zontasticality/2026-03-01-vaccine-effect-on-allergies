# Open Questions — Vaccine Effect on Allergies

## Critical Unanswered Questions

### 1. Why hasn't the whole-cell vs. acellular pertussis RCT been done?
Platts-Mills (2015) explicitly noted that "a proper controlled trial comparing the effects of cellular versus acellular pertussis vaccine on allergic disease has not yet been conducted" [[view in paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/The%20allergy%20epidemics_%201870-2010.html#:~:text=proper%20controlled%20trial%20comparing,has%20not%20yet%20been%20conducted)]. The observational evidence from Australia (Estcourt 2020, 2024) is strong, but an RCT would be definitive. Is there an ethical or practical barrier? Is this being pursued?
- **Update**: A 2024 PLOS Medicine paper was found (immunogenicity trial of mixed wP/aP schedules in Australian infants). Need to check if allergy outcomes were measured.

### 2. What is the actual mechanism by which acellular pertussis increases food allergy risk?
The Estcourt studies show the *association* but the *mechanism* is unclear. Possibilities:
- Acellular pertussis promotes stronger Th2 responses than whole-cell?
- Whole-cell pertussis contains LPS (endotoxin) which promotes Th1 and tolerogenic responses?
- Different adjuvant formulations between wP and aP?
This matters because the mechanism determines what the solution should be.

### 3. Is the food allergy epidemic still accelerating, plateauing, or declining?
The Warren 2021 data is cross-sectional (2015-2016 survey). We need trend data. If the epidemic is plateauing despite continued acellular pertussis use, that weakens the vaccine-causal hypothesis. If it's still rising, multiple causal factors may be at play.

### 4. How much of the allergy epidemic is diagnosis inflation?
The Grüber 2003 study showed that ascertainment bias (unvaccinated children seeing doctors less) explains apparent vaccine-allergy associations. How much of the broader "allergy epidemic" is increased awareness, lower diagnostic thresholds, and the availability of specific IgE testing?

### 5. What is the genetic susceptibility profile for vaccine-related allergy risk?
Terhune & Deth (2018) argue the risk is concentrated in "genetically susceptible subpopulations." If this is true, could genetic screening identify children at elevated risk who might benefit from modified vaccine schedules or formulations?

### 6. Do other vaccines besides DTaP/pertussis show allergy associations?
Most of the strong epidemiological evidence focuses on pertussis vaccine formulation. What about:
- MMR (contains gelatin, chicken fibroblast proteins)
- Influenza (contains ovalbumin)
- Hepatitis B (contains yeast proteins, aluminum adjuvant)
- PCV13/pneumococcal (aluminum phosphate adjuvant)
I did not find controlled comparisons for these individual vaccines.

### 7. What happened in countries that never switched from whole-cell pertussis?
Some countries (especially in the developing world) still use wP. Do they have lower food allergy rates? Confounders would be enormous (diet, hygiene, healthcare access, genetic diversity) but the natural experiment exists.

## Contradictions Found

- The Danish cohort (Gehrt 2021) finds delayed DTaP reduces atopic dermatitis but the Melbourne cohort (Venter 2016) finds NO effect on food allergy from delayed DTaP. These are different outcomes (eczema vs food allergy) so not strictly contradictory, but they suggest DTaP timing affects skin barrier diseases differently from food sensitization.

- The KiGGS study finds no difference in allergy between vaccinated/unvaccinated, while the Estcourt studies find a significant difference between wP/aP. These are not contradictory either — KiGGS compares "any vaccines" vs "none" while Estcourt compares vaccine *formulations*. The signal may be in the formulation, not in vaccination vs. non-vaccination.
