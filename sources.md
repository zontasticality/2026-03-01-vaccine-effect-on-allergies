### 2. Platts-Mills 2015 — The Allergy Epidemics: 1870-2010
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/The%20allergy%20epidemics_%201870-2010.html)
- **Title**: The Allergy Epidemics: 1870-2010
- **Authors**: Thomas A.E. Platts-Mills
- **Journal**: Journal of Allergy and Clinical Immunology, 136(1):3-13
- **Year**: 2015
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jaci.2015.03.048
- **PMID**: 26145982
- **Found via**: User-provided PubMed link (PMID 26145982)
- **Status**: `[downloaded]` `[read-partial: via PMC full text extraction]`
- **Relevance**: **HIGH**. Authoritative review tracing three sequential allergy epidemics. Attributes them primarily to lifestyle changes (hygiene, indoor living, skin exposure). Notes vaccine formulation changes (whole-cell → acellular pertussis in 1992) as potentially relevant to food allergy timing but emphasizes no proper controlled trial exists. Does NOT endorse vaccines as a primary cause.

### 3. Arumugham 2015 — Evidence That Food Proteins in Vaccines Cause the Development of Food Allergies and Its Implications for Vaccine Policy
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/Evidence%20That%20Food%20Proteins%20in%20Vaccines%20Cause%20Food%20Allergies.html)
- **Title**: Evidence That Food Proteins in Vaccines Cause the Development of Food Allergies and Its Implications for Vaccine Policy
- **Authors**: Vinu Arumugham
- **Journal**: Journal of Developing Drugs, 4(4):1000137
- **Year**: 2015
- **DOI**: 10.4172/2329-6631.1000137
- **Found via**: User-provided Scribd link; full text retrieved from Longdom open access
- **Status**: `[downloaded]` `[read-partial: extracted via web fetch]`
- **Relevance**: Central to the hypothesis under investigation. Claims that trace food proteins (ovalbumin, casein, gelatin, soy) in vaccines, combined with aluminum adjuvants, cause IgE-mediated food allergy. Cites Wells 1908 (50ng ovalbumin sensitization in guinea pigs) and 2011 IOM report. Published in a low-impact open-access journal (Longdom/OMICS group — known predatory publisher). The claims are mechanistically plausible but the source quality is poor.

### 4. Terhune et al. 2018 — Aluminum Adjuvant-Containing Vaccines in the Context of the Hygiene Hypothesis: A Risk Factor for Eosinophilia and Allergy in a Genetically Susceptible Subpopulation?
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/Aluminum%20Adjuvant-Containing%20Vaccines.html)
- **Title**: Aluminum Adjuvant-Containing Vaccines in the Context of the Hygiene Hypothesis: A Risk Factor for Eosinophilia and Allergy in a Genetically Susceptible Subpopulation?
- **Authors**: Terhune, Deth
- **Journal**: International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ijerph15050901
- **PMID**: 29751492
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[downloaded]` `[read-partial: via PMC extraction]`
- **Relevance**: **MODERATE-HIGH**. Proposes that aluminum adjuvants trigger Th2-skewed immune responses without regulatory T cell activation, potentially increasing allergy risk in genetically susceptible children. Relies primarily on mouse models. Explicitly acknowledges this is a hypothesis requiring validation, not proven causation. 119 references.

### 5. Mahler et al. 2022 — Anaphylaxis to additives in vaccines
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/Anaphylaxis%20to%20additives%20in%20vaccines.html)
- **Title**: Anaphylaxis to additives in vaccines
- **Authors**: Vera Mahler, Ann-Christine Junker
- **Journal**: Allergo Journal International
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s40629-022-00215-8
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[downloaded]` `[read-partial: via PMC extraction]`
- **Relevance**: **HIGH**. Comprehensive review of allergenic vaccine excipients (gelatin, ovalbumin, casein, PEG, polysorbate 80). Documents anaphylaxis rates of 1-10 per million vaccinations. Key data: ovalbumin limit ≤1 µg/dose, bovine casein detected in nanogram amounts in DTaP. Distinguishes between rare anaphylactic reactions (well-documented) and the broader question of whether vaccines drive population-level allergy sensitization (not addressed).

### 6. Venter et al. 2016 — Timing of routine infant vaccinations and risk of food allergy and eczema at one year of age
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/Timing%20of%20routine%20infant%20vaccinations%20food%20allergy%20eczema.html)

- **DOI**: 10.1111/all.12830
- **PMID**: 26707796

### 8. Estcourt et al. 2020 — Whole-Cell Pertussis Vaccination and Decreased Risk of IgE-Mediated Food Allergy: A Nested Case-Control Study
- **HTML**: [View paper](https://zontasticality.github.io/2026-03-01-vaccine-effect-on-allergies/Whole-Cell%20Pertussis%20Vaccination%20Decreased%20Risk%20Food%20Allergy.html)

- **Journal**: JACI: In Practice, 8(6):2004-2014
- **Year**: 2020
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jaip.2019.12.020
- **PMID**: 31891824
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[downloaded]` `[scouted-only]`

## Additional Sources

### 9. Estcourt et al. 2024 — Infant Whole-Cell Versus Acellular Pertussis Vaccination in 1997 to 1999 and Risk of Childhood Hospitalization for Food-Induced Anaphylaxis
- **Title**: Infant Whole-Cell Versus Acellular Pertussis Vaccination in 1997 to 1999 and Risk of Childhood Hospitalization for Food-Induced Anaphylaxis
- **Authors**: Estcourt et al.
- **Journal**: JACI: In Practice, 12(3):670-680
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jaip.2023.12.048
- **PMID**: 38182097
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[scouted-only]`
- **Relevance**: **VERY HIGH**. Follow-up to #8. 218,093 NSW children. wP recipients had food anaphylaxis rate of 3.5 vs 5.1 per 100,000 child-years (aP); adjusted HR 0.47 (95% CI 0.26-0.83). This is a striking 53% reduction in food anaphylaxis hospitalization with whole-cell vaccine.

### 10. Grüber et al. 2003 — Vaccination and Allergic Disease: A Birth Cohort Study
- **Title**: Vaccination and Allergic Disease: A Birth Cohort Study
- **Authors**: Grüber et al.
- **Journal**: American Journal of Public Health
- **Year**: 2003
- **DOI**: 10.1093/aje/kwh035
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[read-partial: via PMC extraction]`
- **Relevance**: **HIGH**. 29,238 children. Initial vaccine-allergy association vanished after adjusting for GP visit frequency. 83% of unvaccinated children were in the lowest quartile of doctor visits. Concluded the association was ascertainment bias: unvaccinated children simply got diagnosed less.

### 13. Hurley et al. 2018 — Effect of endotoxin and alum adjuvant vaccine on peanut allergy
- **Title**: Effect of endotoxin and alum adjuvant vaccine on peanut allergy
- **Authors**: Hurley, Tadrous
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jaci.2017.09.016
- **Found via**: Web search
- **Status**: `[read-partial: via PMC extraction]`
- **Relevance**: **MODERATE**. Mouse model. Alum/CpG adjuvant combination reduced peanut-induced allergic responses compared to alum alone. Suggests adjuvant formulation modifications could reduce allergy risk. Authors note: "modification of childhood vaccine adjuvant formulations may provide a method to reduce the development/severity of allergic disease."
